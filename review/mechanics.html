The Law of the Conservation of Energy

The Law of the Conservation of Energy is a fundamental theory in physics. This means that their is no proof to it, but we haven't seen anything that breaks this law. Yet.

The Law of the Conservation of Energy states that the total energy in an isolated system remains constant. Energy cannot be created or destroyed, it can only change form.

3. Conservative and Non-Conservative Forces

Imagine you are lifting a box, and you lift it meters in a straight vertical line. Now imagine you are lifting the same box to the same height, but this time you carry it upwards in a wavy pattern. Does one require more work than the other? The answer is no.

On an intuitive level, this makes sense. If both times I'm applying the same upwards force and travelling the same distance, why would one require more work than another?

Now consider pushing a box instead. One time you push it in a straight line, and travel from point to point . Then you push it again, and you also travel from to , but you take a really curvy path. Does one path require more work than the other? The answer this time is actually yes.

This happens because there are non-conservative forces acting on the system. We've only been looking at conservative forces so far, like or without friction. These are forces that do not change the total energy of the system. Non-conservative forces on the other hand change the energy of the system by transferring energy to forms that are not useful.

An example of a non-conservative force is when we push an object. Some of the energy is lost overcoming the force of friction, which turns into heat.
